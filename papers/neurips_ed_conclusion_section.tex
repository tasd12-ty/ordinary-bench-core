\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Ordinary-Bench is built around a simple claim: evaluating spatial intelligence in vision-language models requires more than counting correct answers. By combining controlled 3D-rendered scenes, dense ordinal probing, scene belief reconstruction, and explicit visual-ablation conditions, the benchmark turns scene-level coherence and visual grounding into measurable quantities. This shifts evaluation from ``how many questions did the model answer correctly?'' toward ``what kind of scene belief does the model actually externalize?''

That shift is the main contribution of the benchmark. Even in visually simple, human-intuitive scenes, a model can appear locally competent while failing to support a single coherent geometric interpretation. Ordinary-Bench is designed to make that gap visible and reproducible.
